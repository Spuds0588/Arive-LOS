Overview

In the Arive platform, tasks are frequently underutilized as simple reminders. However, their true potential is unlocked when they are treated as tactical tools integrated directly with your workflow. By linking task completion to specific automations and loan milestones, you can build a robust system that enforces your process, ensures consistent handling of edge-cases, and provides immediate visibility into potential pipeline problems before they escalate, transforming tasks from passive notes to active process drivers.

Key Takeaways

  • Treat Arive tasks as tactical tools for process enforcement, not just simple reminders.
  • Tie task completion to automations and milestones to create a more reliable workflow.
  • Use integrated tasks to instantly identify and address potential pipeline issues.
  • A proper task setup ensures that unique or edge-case scenarios are handled correctly every time.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q: What is the primary mistake users make with tasks in Arive?
    A: The most common mistake is using tasks as standalone reminders rather than integrating them into the system's automation and milestone features to actively enforce and manage the loan process.

    Q: How can connecting tasks to automations improve my loan pipeline?
    A: By linking tasks to automations, you ensure that key steps in the loan process are completed, which helps in identifying bottlenecks and preventing deals from stalling, thereby improving overall pipeline health and efficiency.
